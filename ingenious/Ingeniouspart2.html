Chapter 2

Kari

“Manual Override Successful, Program Initializing.” A computerized feminine voice said. The words also appeared in green lettering on the screen in front of me. The screen went dark and opened. The room I now stood in was filled with mirror walls and a white tiled ceiling and floor.

I had long silky white hair that reached my butt. My eyes were light blue. I wore a red and black checkered long shirt and dark blue pants. I walked forward to the door that would open seconds from now. My ear twitched when the opening doors let in the sound of gun fire in the distance. I'm not used to the enhanced senses so I can't tell how far, but it's most likely at the control center. I ran down the long hallway to my testing room. In my testing room the monitor had a long math equation with a 30 second timer.

“They should've made this harder.” I sighed as I calculated the answer instantly. I wrote the answer with my finger. The monitor tuned off and split apart to show the next hallway. As I ran towards the control center the gun shots got louder, mostly, and my head started to pound. It wasn't just the noise that was giving me a headache, it was everything about my new body; the enhanced eyesight kept going out of control, the noise would randomly get louder and softer, I would randomly use too my strength in my steps making it uneven, and the worst part was the time distortion I felt. If you were to move faster, time moves relatively slower since time is only in a person's head. All of it was making me sick so I stopped for a moment.

“Excuse me, are you Master Kari?” A soldier put his hand on my shoulder while I was trying to control my senses.

“Don't touch me!” I grabbed his hand and pulled it off my shoulder. I used to my strength in my grip and heard the bones in his arm crack. He screamed in pain. I quickly let go and covered my ears. “Don't do that!”

“You broke my arm!” He held his arm in the other.

“That was your fault and stop yelling!” I was annoyed. I started running toward the control center again.

“Wait, not that way!” He called out.

“What is it now?” I asked.

“Our troops are losing the battle down there. There's another way out of here isn't there?” He asked.

“Of course there is.” I gave him a stupid look, “Throw me your gun.” I commanded.

“What? Why?” He asked.

“So I can help you idiots.” He paused then handed it to me. I wasn't familiar with the model since it's been a number of years, but it looked like a machine gun. “How many bullets are left?”

“There should be about twelve in there.” He answered.

“About or are?” I said.

“Twelve.” He shook his head.

“Throw me a knife.” He passed me his switch blade. He followed me down the hall until we reached the exit. There was a lot of gunfire and yelling. I had already learned to control my senses enough to stand the gunfire. A few of the soldiers by the exit had noticed us, but were still focusing on the battle. “I hope they're wearing the same uniform as you or else I might kill them.” I said launching myself into the fight.

“Hey wait!” The soldier called after me, but I already made up my mind. I ran right out and shot the soldiers on the opposite side on sight. I counted eighteen live enemy soldiers. I pulled the trigger and let loose a volley of bullets, re-aiming in the fraction of a second that I had between the shots as I dodged the incoming bullets. I missed once, but the other soldiers helped and managed to shoot six of the other targets. I threw the empty gun at the last soldier as I dashed toward him. I stabbed him in the neck as I dodged the final bullet.

“I win.” I said as I put down the blade. “So, how long has it been since I was awake?” I asked. Only one person stepped forward. He was older than the others and seemed to be more confident.

“You've been asleep for 30 years.” He answered.

“I was supposed to be asleep for 50, what happened?” I asked.

“The virus has gotten out of hand. Back in your time there were a bit over nine billion people and we had a surplus of humans. Then that virus came and-,” He started explaining.

“And destroyed 43% the world's population in just a year, yeah I know all that. I went to sleep back in 2054. I have eidetic memory so I don't forget anything. It was part of the plan just in case the others lost their memory. What's the real reason you woke us up?” I asked.

“Two years after you left, there was a second strain of the virus transmitted by any bodily fluid. It made the destruction even faster. We have less than 900 thousand people left. That's less than .01% left.” He answered.

“Interesting, but he planned for something like this.” I said. “I'm tired, get me back to your base and we'll talk some more. I want to rendezvous with the others soon.” I walked out with them.
